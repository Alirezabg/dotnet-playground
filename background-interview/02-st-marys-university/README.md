# St Mary's University — Likely Questions

Questions about your degree and academic background at St Mary's University. Interviewers use these to gauge **fundamentals, self-direction, and how you learn**.

## Your degree
1. What did you study at St Mary's, and why did you choose it?
2. Which modules did you find most valuable for software engineering?
3. How did your degree prepare you for a .NET engineering role?
4. What was your final-year project / dissertation? Walk me through it.
5. What technologies did you use in your academic projects?

## Project / dissertation deep-dive
6. What problem did your dissertation solve? Why did it matter?
7. What was the hardest part, and how did you overcome it?
8. If you did it again, what would you do differently?
9. How did you validate that your solution worked?
10. Did you work solo or in a team? How was the work divided?

## Fundamentals (expect follow-ups from CS modules)
11. Explain a data structure or algorithm you learned and where you'd apply it.
12. What did you learn about software design / architecture academically?
13. How do you bridge the gap between academic theory and industry practice?

## Learning & growth
14. University is a few years back — how have you kept your skills current since?
15. What's the most important thing your degree taught you that wasn't about code?
16. How do you teach yourself a new technology now?
